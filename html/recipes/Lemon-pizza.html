Lemon Pizza

Description

This bright and zesty pizza features thin slices of fresh lemon layered over creamy mozzarella, smoky gouda, and tons of fresh basil. Baked on a homemade crust, this pie can be cooked in the oven or finished on a smoker for an unforgettable twist.

Ingredients

Homemade Pizza Dough

  • 2¼ tsp active dry yeast (1 packet)
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • ¾ cup warm water (110°F)
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp olive oil

Toppings

  • 1 large lemon, seeded and thinly sliced into paper-thin rounds
  • 6–8 oz fresh mozzarella, sliced
  • ½ cup shredded smoked gouda
  • 1 large handful fresh basil leaves
  • Olive oil, for drizzling
  • Cracked black pepper, to taste

Instructions

1. Make the Dough

  1. In a small bowl, combine yeast, sugar, and warm water. Let sit for 5–10 minutes until foamy.
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine flour and salt. Add yeast mixture and olive oil. Mix and knead until smooth and elastic (about 8 minutes).
  3. Cover and let rise in a warm place for 1 hour, or until doubled in size.

2. Prep the Toppings

  1. Wash the lemon and slice very thinly. Remove all seeds. Pat slices dry with a paper towel.
  2. Slice the mozzarella and shred the smoked gouda. Wash and dry the basil leaves.

3. Assemble the Pizza

  1. Preheat your oven to 475°F (or preheat smoker with pizza stone to medium-high heat).
  2. Roll out the dough on a floured surface into a 10–12 inch round. Transfer to a pizza stone or baking sheet.
  3. Arrange lemon slices in a single layer across the dough.
  4. Top with slices of mozzarella and a sprinkle of smoked gouda.
  5. Scatter whole basil leaves generously across the top.
  6. Drizzle with a bit of olive oil and season with cracked black pepper.

4. Bake or Smoke

  1. Oven method: Bake for 10–14 minutes, or until crust is golden and cheese is bubbling.
  2. Smoker method: Place on preheated smoker with stone and cook covered for 10–15 minutes until golden and lightly smoky.

To Serve

Let pizza rest for 5 minutes before slicing. Serve warm with an extra drizzle of olive oil or a few torn fresh basil leaves.
